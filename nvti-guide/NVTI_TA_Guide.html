Overview

The National Veteran Training Institute (NVTI), run by Management Concepts, is a federally funded training center in Dallas, Texas. All staff are being asked to attend in person in 2026 — both the DVOP Core Competency and LVER Core Competency courses are encouraged.

Because this is third-party funded training, the TA process in Edison is simplified to a single expense line. The steps below will walk you through everything from registering to getting approved to leave the state.

Key Rule Everything at NVTI is paid for by DOL VETS/Management Concepts — hotel, meals, training. The only costs you submit for reimbursement are your travel from home to the airport and back (and parking if applicable). Those go on your expense report after you return, not on the TA.
1

Before You Start

You need two things before you can fully submit your TA. You can set everything up ahead of time, but do not hit Submit until both are in hand.

A
Your Flight Itinerary (or Price Quote)

Management Concepts will send you a flight itinerary approximately 3–5 weeks before your NVTI date. If you're driving, they'll send a price quote showing what the equivalent flight would cost. Keep this email — it's your itinerary attachment.

Driving is allowed, but reimbursement is capped at the cost of the equivalent flight. If mileage exceeds the flight cost, you cover the difference.

B
Signed Memorandum of Authorization (Daniel handles this)

Daniel will submit a memo request to the Assistant Commissioner (AC) for approval once you forward him your itinerary. The signed memo authorizes you to leave the state on state business. You must have this before submitting in Edison.

Email Daniel your itinerary as soon as you receive it. He will handle the rest and route the signed memo back to you.

Do Not Leave Without Approval Your TA must be fully approved before you travel. If something happens and you were not approved to leave the state, you are not covered. Keep an eye on your TA status daily as your travel date approaches and contact Jeff or Daniel immediately if it hasn't moved.
Fiscal Year Cutoff — Important The state runs a July–June fiscal calendar. If your NVTI date is in or after July, do not submit your TA until we get closer to that date. Finance reconciles around May–June, and TAs sitting past June may need to be closed out. When in doubt, check with Jeff or Daniel before submitting.
2

Register for Your NVTI Course

Each person registers individually on the NVTI website. Do this first, before any Edison steps.

1
Go to nvti.org and create your account

Each staff member needs their own account. If you need help with the online registration system, email student services: studentservices@nvti.org (contact name: Alexandra).

2
Select your course and preferred dates

Browse available sessions under NVTI Class Schedule → NVTI Training in Dallas. Look for the DVOP Core Competency and/or LVER Core Competency in-person sessions. The current schedule runs through September — a new schedule will be released July/August once federal funding is confirmed for October onward.

3
Complete the registration form — include supervisor emails

The form will ask for supervisor email addresses. Include:

Wendy Harrison (DVETS)  ·  Jeff  ·  Daniel  ·  Your Team Lead or AD

This ensures everyone gets automatically CC'd on your request and confirmation. For ADs/Regionals: including Daniel ensures your memo gets processed correctly.

4
Look for your confirmation

You and your listed supervisors will receive a confirmation email. Once confirmed, NVTI will send you pre-class materials and your itinerary 3–5 weeks out. At that point, forward the itinerary to Daniel to kick off the memo process.

Cross-Training Encouraged DVOPs are encouraged to also attend a LVER Core Competency session, and LVERs to attend DVOP — and vice versa. This helps you understand what your counterparts are learning. Space permitting, you can attend either course. The September LVER class tends to fill quickly, so plan early.
3

Create Your Travel Authorization in Edison

You can build out the TA ahead of time and save it without submitting. Only hit Submit once you have your itinerary and Daniel's signed memo.

1
Navigate to: Travel and Expense → Create Travel Authorization

Log in to Edison and go to the Travel and Expense module.

2
Description

Enter: Out of Service Training (NVTI)

3
Business Purpose

Enter: Out of Service Training

4
Comments

Enter: Federal mandated training from DOL VETS

5
Travel From — use the Lookup Tool

Click the magnifying glass (Lookup Tool). Under Search Criteria → Description, type your city/location in Tennessee. Select the result that matches your departure city (e.g., "Clarksville Montgomery County").

If your city doesn't appear, call the Edison helpdesk or submit a request through Edison to have your location added.

6
Travel To

Type Dallas in the Lookup Tool search. Select: Texas — Dallas, Dallas

7
Travel Dates

Enter your travel start and end dates (e.g., Sunday departure through Friday return). The system will auto-calculate the number of days.

8
Add ONE Expense Line — Registration Fee

Because the entire trip is third-party paid, you only need a single line. Edison's travel manual confirms this. Select Registration Fee.

Description field:

Mandatory training paid for by DOL VETS — National Veteran Training Institute (NVTI)

Amount: $0.01  (one cent)

Then click Details → select Cash → Done.

The one cent amount is per the Edison travel manual for third-party funded trips. Finance is familiar with this — if they question it, refer them to the Travel Authorization section of the travel manual covering third-party funding.

9
Save — do not Submit yet

Hit Save. This stores your TA without sending it for approval. Come back to Submit once you have all required documents attached (see Step 4).

4

Required Documents to Attach

Attach all of the following before you hit Submit. Daniel provides the memo; you gather the rest.

Document Who Provides It Notes
Flight Itinerary
Required		 NVTI sends it ~3–5 weeks out. Forward to Daniel. If driving, use the price quote email NVTI sends showing the equivalent flight cost.
Signed Memorandum of Authorization
Required		 Daniel submits to AC after you send him the itinerary. Signed memo comes back to you. Must be signed by the Assistant Commissioner before you submit in Edison.
Course Authorization Document
Required		 Jeff provides — DVOP or LVER Core Competency doc, or Leadership/Integration doc depending on your course. Jeff will send these to the team. Hold them until you're ready to submit.
Ethics Memorandum
Required		 Finance sends it to you automatically after TA submission. Per executive order — receiving in-kind funds from NVTI requires an ethics acknowledgment. Don't panic when you get it. Forward to Jeff or Daniel, they'll sign and return it to finance.
5

Submit & Track Approval

1
Upload all documents, then hit Submit

Attach your itinerary, signed memo, and course auth doc. Then submit. The TA will go to your AD/Team Lead for first approval.

2
AD reviews and approves

Your supervisor will review the TA and approve it in Edison.

3
Fiscal reviews and approves

Finance will do a final review. They may request additional lines if they need more detail — Jeff will notify you if anything changes. If they kick it back, you can use the Withdrawal button to pull it back, make changes, and resubmit.

✓
Approved — you're cleared to travel

Check Edison daily as your travel date approaches. You must have full approval before leaving the state. If it hasn't moved and your trip is close, contact Jeff or Daniel immediately.

HR Approval Note The memo goes through HR at the higher levels before AC signs. This process can take time. In some cases approval came through only days before departure. Stay in communication — don't wait until the last minute to flag that something looks stuck.
6

After You Return — Expense Report

When you get back, cancel your TA and create a new Expense Report for your personal out-of-pocket travel costs (home to airport and back, parking).

1
Cancel your Travel Authorization in Edison

Go back to your saved TA and cancel it. This keeps finance's books clean since the trip has been completed and the third-party costs don't need reconciliation.

2
Create a new Travel Expense Report

Go to Travel and Expense → Create Expense Report. Use your actual travel dates (not future dates — Edison won't allow future dates on expense reports).

3
Add your personal expense lines

Include: In-state point-to-point mileage (home to airport, airport to home) and parking receipt if you parked at the airport. Re-upload your supporting documents.

Use the Quick Field or Add Expense Line options. Edison has pre-loaded templates like "Out-of-State Mileage" that you can select to speed this up.

4
Submit your Expense Report

Submit for reimbursement. A session covering the expense report process will be scheduled once Terrell returns from his trip — watch for that training invite.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drive instead of fly?
Yes. You're authorized to drive to Dallas. However, reimbursement is capped at the equivalent flight cost. NVTI will send a price quote showing the flight cost; use that as your "itinerary" attachment. If your mileage exceeds the flight cost, you cover the difference (typically a small amount).
Can I fly out on Sunday or stay the weekend after training?
Yes. If the flight cost is the same, NVTI will approve a Sunday departure or a Saturday/Sunday return. Let NVTI know when you register. Hotel stays for personal weekend time are at your own expense. Training typically ends Friday morning with afternoon flights.
My NVTI date isn't until August — should I submit my TA now?
No. Wait until closer to your date. The state fiscal year ends in June; TAs sitting past June may need to be closed out during reconciliation. Jeff and Daniel will notify the team when it's the right time to submit for later dates.
Do I need to take out my laptop at airport security?
Rules vary by line and change frequently. If TSA asks you to take it out, just pull it out separately. Nashville's airport has expanded enough that you may not need to — but be prepared just in case.
What if I'm new and haven't attended NVTI at all?
Start with the DVOP Core Competency (or LVER Core Competency depending on your role) as your first in-person course. There are also online prerequisites and micro-learnings available immediately on nvti.org. Follow the NVTI Journey path for a structured roadmap from week one through 24 months.
Can DVOPs attend LVER courses and vice versa?
Yes — space permitting. The courses are similar but understanding what your counterpart learns builds a better team. Both DVOPs and LVERs are encouraged to cross-attend in 2026. Note: the September LVER class fills fast.
Can Wagner-Pizer staff attend NVTI?
Yes — as of 2026 Wagner-Pizer staff are being welcomed back. The process for them is similar. Reach out to Daniel to ensure the memo process is handled correctly.
Where do I find Edison's travel manual if I want to reference the rules myself?
In Edison: Travel and Expense → Create Travel Authorization → click the three dots (⋯) on the right → Help. This opens a full library of travel user guides including third-party funding TA instructions, expense report guides, and supervisor/approver guides.
